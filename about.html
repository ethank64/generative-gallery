About

I started programming when I was about 14, and p5.js is where it all clicked.

When I first started learning to code, I remember binge-watching Daniel Shiffman's tutorials and coding challenges on The Coding Train. His boundless enthusiasm turned abstract ideas into something fun and made the intimidating parts feel within reach.

Recently I came across a generative-art skill in Claude Code that builds with p5.js, and it brought all of those memories back. It's what inspired this project: a digital art gallery to show the beauty and power of the p5.js library — the same library that taught me to program.

Every piece here runs live in the browser. Change a seed, pull a sketch apart, and make it your own. And if you're just starting out, go watch The Coding Train — thanks, Dan.
